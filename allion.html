TOYOTA

Allion
  • Description
  • Service Kit
  • Reviews

The Toyota Allion is a Japanese subcompact that has been in production for three generations since 2004. It is a basic five-seater, five-door hatchback. The first generation Passo (M300) was in production from 2004 to 2010. The second generation (M600) was in production from 2010 to 2016, whilst the third (M700) is current. The Passo competes in the same over-subscribed sub-compact space, differentiating itself on styling, space, and fuel economy (18km per litre). The car is also regarded as low maintenance, due to the reliability and simplicity of the engines. The 2012 Toyota Passo was offered with two engine options. These are the 1.0L 1KR-FE (3 cylinder) and the 1.3L 1NR-FE (4 cylinder) engine. These engines are also used on the Toyota Vitz. These are linked to either a CVT or a manual transmission, and available in both 2WD and 4WD. The car offers adequate space for both luggage and passengers for short distances. Height is average such that a tall driver may be less comfortable. Extra boot space can be provided by folding the rear seats. Autoheads advises owners to take care of their cars by getting the correct spares from reputable traders, as well as enlisting the services of qualified mechanics. Autoheads helps in ensuring that your car stays on the road.
